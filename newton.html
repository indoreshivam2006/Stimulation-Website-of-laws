Newton's Laws of Motion

1st Law: Law of Inertia

Explanation:

"An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion continues in motion with the same speed and direction unless acted upon by an external force."

Mathematical Formulation:

Since there is no acceleration (a = 0), Newton's first law is expressed as:

F = 0

where F is the net external force. If no external force is applied, an object will not change its state of motion. This means a stationary object will remain still, and a moving object will keep moving at a constant velocity. The tendency of an object to resist a change in its state of motion is called inertia.

2nd Law: Law of Acceleration

Explanation:

"The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force acting on it and inversely proportional to its mass."

Mathematical Formulation:

F = m a

F = Force (Newton)

m = Mass (kg)

a = Acceleration(m/s2)

A heavier object requires more force to accelerate than a lighter object. A car accelerates faster when more force is applied to the gas pedal

3rd Law: Action and Reaction

Explanation:

"For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction."

Mathematical Formulation:

FAB = -FBA

If object A exerts a force FAB on object B, then object B exerts an equal and opposite force FBA on object A
