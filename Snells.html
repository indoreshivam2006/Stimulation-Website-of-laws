Snell's Law

Explanation:

"Snell's law states that when a wave passes from one medium to another, the ratio of the sine of the angle of incidence to the sine of the angle of refraction is a constant. This law is also known as the law of refraction. "

How does Snell's law work ?

• When light passes from one medium to another, it bends or refracts.

• The amount of bending depends on the refractive index of the media involved.

• The refractive index is a constant value that depends on the color of light and the pair of media.

History of Snell's law

• The Dutch mathematician and astronomer Willebrord Snell discovered the law in 1621.

• René Descartes published the law in 1637.

• Snell's law is also known as the Snell-Descartes law, the ibn-Sahl law, and the law of refraction.

Applications of Snell's law.

• Snell's law is used in ray tracing.

• It's used in experimental optics to find the refractive index of a material.
